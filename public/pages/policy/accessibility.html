Accessibility

Our commitment to inclusion and universal accessibility

1. Our commitment

At SWAPP-IT, we believe that education should be accessible to everyone. We are committed to making our platform inclusive and usable by people with diverse abilities, following web accessibility best practices and WCAG 2.1 AA guidelines.

Our goal: To provide an equitable user experience, regardless of our users' physical, cognitive, or technological abilities.

2. Accessibility standards

Our platform is developed following these standards and guidelines:

  • WCAG 2.1 AA: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines level AA
  • Section 508: Accessibility standards for information technology
  • ADA: Americans with Disabilities Act
  • International standards: Compliance with global accessibility regulations

3. Implemented accessibility features

Navigation and structure

  • Semantic structure: Proper use of headings (H1, H2, H3) to organize content
  • Keyboard navigation: All functionality is accessible via keyboard
  • Descriptive links: Clear and descriptive link text
  • Breadcrumbs: Breadcrumb navigation for orientation

Visual content

  • Color contrast: Minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for normal text
  • Alternative text: All images have alternative descriptions
  • Resizable text: Content adapts to zoom up to 200%
  • Readable fonts: Clear typography with adequate size

Interactivity

  • Large click areas: Buttons and links with minimum size of 44x44 pixels
  • Focus states: Clear visual indicators for focused elements
  • Accessible forms: Labels properly associated with fields
  • Error messages: Clear descriptions of errors and how to correct them

4. Compatible assistive technologies

Our platform is compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen readers: NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, TalkBack
  • Voice recognition software: Dragon NaturallySpeaking, Voice Control
  • Browsers with zoom: Integrated zoom functionality in all browsers
  • Magnification software: ZoomText, MAGic
  • Alternative input devices: Switches, adaptive keyboards

5. Specific features for users with disabilities

For users with visual disabilities

  • Full compatibility with screen readers
  • Descriptive alternative text for all images
  • Optimized color contrast
  • Intuitive keyboard navigation

For users with hearing disabilities

  • Subtitles on all videos (when applicable)
  • Audio content transcripts
  • Visual indicators for important notifications
  • Primarily text-based communication

For users with motor disabilities

  • Complete keyboard navigation
  • Large and well-spaced click areas
  • Autocomplete functionality in forms
  • Alternative input options

For users with cognitive disabilities

  • Clear and consistent interface
  • Step-by-step instructions
  • Clear and constructive error messages
  • Interface customization options

6. Accessibility testing

We conduct regular accessibility testing using:

  • Automated tools: WAVE, axe-core, Lighthouse
  • Manual testing: Evaluation by accessibility experts
  • User testing: Feedback from users with disabilities
  • External audits: Evaluations by specialized third parties

7. Continuous improvements

We are committed to continuously improving the accessibility of our platform:

  • Regular updates: Review and improvement of existing features
  • New features: Development of new features with integrated accessibility
  • User feedback: Incorporation of community suggestions
  • Team training: Continuous training in accessibility best practices

8. Known limitations

Although we strive to make our platform fully accessible, we recognize that some limitations may exist:

  • Third-party content: Some external elements may not meet our standards
  • New features: Recently launched features may require adjustments
  • Older browsers: Some features may not be available in very old versions

We actively work to resolve these limitations and continuously improve the experience.
